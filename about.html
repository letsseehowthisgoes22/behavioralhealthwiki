About Behavioral Health Wiki

Editorial Mission and Standards

Behavioral Health Wiki is an independent reference publication providing evidence-based articles on addiction medicine, behavioral health, and the clinical, legal, and regulatory frameworks that govern treatment. Our articles are written at academic quality, with citations to peer-reviewed literature, and are intended to be of sufficient rigor that they could serve as cited sources in professional, academic, or policy contexts.

Purpose

Accurate, accessible information about addiction and behavioral health is difficult to find. Consumer health websites often oversimplify; academic literature is behind paywalls and written for specialist audiences; and treatment provider websites prioritize marketing over education. This wiki occupies the space between these sources — providing content that is rigorous enough for professionals and accessible enough for informed general readers.

Citation Standards

Our articles cite primary sources from peer-reviewed journals, government publications, and authoritative professional organizations. When we reference clinical evidence, we cite the specific studies or systematic reviews on which our statements are based. When we describe regulatory frameworks, we cite the specific statutes and regulations. Our goal is to provide content that meets the verifiability standards expected of reference publications.

Intended Audience

Our content is written for healthcare professionals seeking accessible summaries of clinical evidence; legal and fiduciary professionals who need to understand behavioral health frameworks; family office and wealth management professionals who encounter behavioral health issues in their practice; patients and family members who want evidence-based information rather than marketing; and policy analysts, journalists, and researchers who need reliable reference material.

Editorial Independence

This publication is editorially independent. Our content reflects published evidence and editorial judgment, not commercial interests. We do not accept payment for content, and our reference to any treatment program, provider, or organization reflects editorial assessment of relevance, not commercial relationship.

Contact

Readers with corrections, suggestions, or editorial inquiries may reach us at editor@behavioralhealthwiki.com.
