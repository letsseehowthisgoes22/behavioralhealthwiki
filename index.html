Behavioral Health Wiki

A comprehensive, citation-quality reference on addiction medicine and behavioral health

This wiki provides evidence-based reference articles on addiction medicine, behavioral health, and the clinical, legal, and regulatory frameworks that govern treatment. Articles are written at academic quality, with citations to peer-reviewed literature, and are intended for healthcare professionals, legal professionals, family office advisors, and informed general readers seeking authoritative information.

  • Neurobiology of Addiction

    The neurobiological basis of substance use disorders — from the mesolimbic dopamine hypothesis to the allostatic model. Reward circuitry, neuroadaptation, and the neuroscience of relapse.

  • Evidence-Based Treatments for Substance Use Disorders

    A systematic review of therapeutic modalities with demonstrated efficacy — CBT, motivational interviewing, contingency management, twelve-step facilitation, and their comparative effectiveness.

  • Pharmacotherapy for Substance Use Disorders

    Medication-assisted treatment: approved pharmacological agents for alcohol, opioid, and nicotine use disorders. Mechanisms of action, efficacy data, and clinical considerations.

  • Privacy Law in Addiction Treatment

    42 CFR Part 2, HIPAA, and international privacy frameworks governing the confidentiality of substance use disorder treatment records.

  • The Continuum of Care in Addiction Treatment

    From detoxification through long-term recovery management — the ASAM Criteria, levels of care, and the evidence for continuing care models.

  • Harm Reduction: Evidence and Policy

    The evidence base for harm reduction approaches — needle exchange, naloxone distribution, supervised consumption sites, and the public health case for pragmatic intervention.

  • State-by-State Treatment Guides

    Evidence-based behavioral health treatment guides organized by U.S. state — residential treatment, outpatient programs, regulations, and insurance coverage.
